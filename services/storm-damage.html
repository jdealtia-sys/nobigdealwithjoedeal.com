Joe Deal has worked alongside insurance adjusters on hundreds of storm damage claims in Greater Cincinnati. He knows what adjusters look for, how to document damage thoroughly, and how to make sure nothing legitimate gets missed or undervalued in the initial inspection. We handle roof, siding, and gutter storm damage — often all three on the same home after a major hail or wind event. Emergency tarping is available for active roof leaks while the claim process works through. We work with all major insurance carriers operating in Ohio.

Questions & Answers

Storm Damage & Insurance FAQ

Do I need to file a claim before calling you?
No — call Joe first. He'll inspect the damage and tell you honestly whether you have a claim worth filing. Filing unnecessarily can affect your rates, so it's worth knowing what you're working with before you contact your insurer.
How soon after a storm should I get my roof inspected?
As soon as possible. Insurance policies have claim filing deadlines, and documentation is always stronger while the damage is fresh. If you had a major storm in your area, call Joe and get on the schedule.
What does your storm inspection cover?
We inspect the full exterior — roof field, ridgeline, flashing, gutters, downspouts, siding, windows, AC unit top, and any other impact-susceptible surfaces. We're looking for hail strikes, wind damage, and impact marks that adjusters need to see.
What if the insurance adjuster's estimate is lower than your repair estimate?
This happens. We can supplement the claim — which means providing documentation and line-item support for damage that was missed or undervalued in the adjuster's estimate. The insurance company isn't always wrong, but they're also not always thorough.
What insurance companies do you work with?
We work with all major carriers operating in Ohio — State Farm, Nationwide, Grange, Erie, Allstate, Progressive, and others. The process is similar regardless of carrier.
Is storm damage restoration covered by my deductible?
Your deductible applies to insurance claims regardless of the repair type. We can't waive or absorb your deductible — contractors who offer to do that are operating outside Ohio law. We make sure you're getting the maximum legitimate payout so your out-of-pocket is as low as it can honestly be.
