    Siding Repair

    Siding Repair Done
    Without the Upsell

    Not every siding problem needs a full replacement. Cracked panels, missing sections, storm-damaged areas, gaps around trim — these are often targeted repairs that cost a fraction of tearing everything off and starting over.

    The challenge with siding repair is matching existing material. Different manufacturers, discontinued colors, fading over time — it takes experience to source close matches and execute repairs that blend in well. That's work we do regularly across Greater Cincinnati.

    If your siding was damaged by hail, wind, or impact, there's a real chance your homeowner's insurance covers the repair. Joe will inspect the damage, document it thoroughly, and walk you through whether a claim makes sense before you do anything.

    • Individual panel replacement — sourced to match existing siding as closely as possible
    • Storm and hail damage repair — section by section
    • Trim and corner post repair
    • Gap sealing and caulking around windows, doors, and penetrations
    • Impact damage repair — dents, cracks, and holes
    • Moisture intrusion repair behind compromised panels
    • Insurance claim documentation for covered damage
    • 5-year labor warranty on all repair work

    Questions & Answers

    Siding Repair FAQ

    Can you match my existing siding color and style?
    We do our best to source close matches. Exact matches depend on manufacturer availability and how much the existing siding has faded. We'll show you samples before any work starts so there are no surprises.
    My siding has a few cracked panels — does that mean I need to replace all of it?
    Not necessarily. If the underlying structure is solid and the damage is isolated, panel replacement is usually the right call. Joe will give you an honest assessment — not a pitch for a full job he doesn't think you need.
    Does insurance cover siding repairs from a storm?
    If the damage was caused by a covered weather event, yes. Hail and wind damage are frequently covered. The key is proper documentation of the damage. Joe handles that process regularly.
    How long does a siding repair typically take?
    Most targeted repairs take anywhere from a few hours to a full day depending on how much area is involved. We'll give you a realistic time estimate upfront.
    Can loose or warped siding cause bigger problems if I ignore it?
    Yes. Gaps and loose panels let moisture into the wall cavity, which can lead to insulation damage, mold, and eventually structural issues. It's worth getting it looked at sooner rather than later.
