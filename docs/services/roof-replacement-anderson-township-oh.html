Common questions

Roof Replacement FAQ — Anderson Township

How long does a roof replacement take in Anderson Township? ▸
Most Anderson Township residential roof replacements are completed in one to two days, depending on the size and complexity of the roofline. Larger homes with multiple dormers and penetrations — common in this area — may take an additional day. Joe gives you a clear timeline before work begins.
Does my homeowner's insurance cover roof replacement? ▸
It depends on the cause. Storm or hail damage is typically covered under standard Ohio homeowner's policies. Age-related wear is generally not. Joe inspects your roof first and gives you an honest assessment of whether a claim is worth pursuing before you file anything.
What roofing materials are best for Anderson Township homes? ▸
GAF architectural shingles are the most common choice — they match the look of the area's established housing stock and carry strong manufacturer warranties. Impact-resistant options are worth considering given Ohio's hail exposure. Joe walks you through every option with honest pros and cons.
Do you handle permits for roof replacement in Anderson Township? ▸
Yes. Anderson Township is in Hamilton County and falls under Ohio building code requirements for roofing work. Joe's crew pulls all required permits and handles inspections so you don't have to manage any of that paperwork.
