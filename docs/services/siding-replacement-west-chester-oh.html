Common questions

Siding Replacement FAQ — West Chester

How do I know if my West Chester home needs siding replacement vs. repair? ▸
Repair makes sense when damage is isolated to a few panels — a cracked section, lifted J-channel, or a gap at one window. Replacement is better when: damage covers more than 20–25% of a wall, panels are faded or brittle throughout, moisture has gotten behind the siding and compromised the house wrap, or hail damage is widespread enough to support an insurance claim. Joe inspects the full envelope and gives you a straight answer.
What siding material does NBD install? ▸
Vinyl is the most common — durable, low-maintenance, and available in hundreds of colors and profiles. Fiber cement (James Hardie) offers a more solid feel and is excellent for homes that see heavy moisture or UV exposure. LP SmartSide is a treated wood composite that holds paint well and resists impact better than standard vinyl. Joe can walk you through the trade-offs for your specific home and budget.
Does homeowners insurance cover siding replacement in West Chester? ▸
When a storm causes the damage, yes — hail, wind, and falling debris are covered perils under standard homeowners policies. The key is documenting the damage correctly. Joe photographs impact patterns, measures dent/crack density, and presents the findings in the format adjusters expect. The southwest storm corridor that runs through Butler County delivers hail events that crack vinyl and dent aluminum at a frequency that makes West Chester homeowners regular insurance claimants — Joe knows how to position the documentation to get full replacement approved. If the cause is wear and tear rather than storm damage, he'll tell you that upfront.
How long does siding replacement take? ▸
A typical single-family home takes 2–4 days depending on square footage, number of penetrations (windows, doors, vents), and material. Fiber cement takes longer than vinyl due to cutting and fastening requirements. Joe sequences the job to keep the home weathered at the end of each day — no open walls overnight.
