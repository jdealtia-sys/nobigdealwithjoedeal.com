Common questions

Roof Repair FAQ — Batavia

How do I know if my Batavia roof needs repair or full replacement? ▸
Roof repair makes sense when damage is isolated — a few missing shingles, a cracked pipe boot, lifted flashing at one valley — and the surrounding shingles still have granule coverage and structural integrity. Full replacement is better when more than 25–30% of the surface is affected or when shingles are at end of life. Joe does a free inspection and gives you a straight answer either way — no upselling.
What does a roof repair inspection include? ▸
Joe goes on the roof (not a drive-by assessment), checks every penetration point (chimney, vents, skylights), inspects flashing at all valleys and transitions, looks for lifted or missing ridge cap, checks for granule loss patterns that indicate age-related wear, and does an attic moisture check if access is available. You get photo documentation of every finding.
Does homeowners insurance cover roof repairs in Batavia? ▸
It depends on the cause. Storm-related damage (hail impact, wind uplift) is typically covered under your homeowners policy subject to your deductible. Wear-and-tear repairs generally aren't. Joe can help document the damage properly if there's an insurance angle — and if it's maintenance-related, he'll tell you upfront so you know what to expect.
How long does a typical roof repair take? ▸
Most repairs are a half-day to full-day job depending on scope. Shingle repairs, pipe boot replacements, and minor flashing work can usually be completed in one trip. Larger flashing repairs or areas with decking rot may take a full day. Joe schedules with real windows, not four-hour arrival ranges.
