Common questions

Roof Inspection FAQ — Batavia

Is a free roof inspection worth it for my Batavia home? ▸
Yes — specifically because Joe goes on the roof. A drive-by or satellite assessment misses flashing failures, lifted ridge cap, and penetration damage that's invisible from ground level. You get photo documentation of every finding, a written summary, and an honest recommendation. If your roof is fine, he tells you. If it needs attention, you know exactly what and why.
How long does a roof inspection take? ▸
Most inspections take 45–90 minutes depending on roof size, complexity (number of penetrations, valleys, pitches), and findings. Joe also checks gutters and fascia as part of the same visit. You get the photo report the same day — not a call-back with a summary two days later.
When should I get my Batavia roof inspected? ▸
After any significant hail or wind event is the most important trigger — hail damage isn't always visible from the ground, and missing it means missing the insurance claim window (typically 1–2 years from the event). Beyond storms, every 3–5 years is a reasonable maintenance cadence for roofs in the Clermont County area, or any time you notice ceiling stains, granules in the gutters, or lifted shingles.
What does the inspection report include? ▸
Joe provides a written summary covering: overall shingle condition and estimated remaining life, specific areas of damage or concern with photos, flashing condition at all chimneys/vents/skylights, gutters and fascia, attic ventilation assessment if access is available, and a clear recommendation — repair, replace, or check back in X years. No padding, no manufactured urgency.
