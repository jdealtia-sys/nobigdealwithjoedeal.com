Common Questions

What Homeowners Ask
About Hail Claims.

Will filing a claim raise my insurance rates?

In Ohio, insurance companies cannot raise your rates for a single weather-related claim — it's an act of nature, not negligence. That said, every policy is different. Joe will tell you upfront if your deductible situation makes filing worth it or not.

My neighbor got their roof done — does that mean I have damage too?

Often yes, but not always. Hail damage depends on which direction the storm came from, the pitch of your roof, and the age of your shingles. Joe will give you an honest answer — if there's no viable claim, he'll tell you that too.

How long do I have to file a hail damage claim in Ohio?

Most Ohio policies allow 1-2 years from the date of loss. However, waiting too long makes the documentation harder and adjusters more skeptical. If you had a storm in the last 18 months, call Joe now and find out what you're working with.

What if my claim gets denied?

Denials get appealed. Joe documents everything specifically so there's a paper trail. If the initial denial is bogus, there are options — including Ohio's appraisal process. He's been through this before.
