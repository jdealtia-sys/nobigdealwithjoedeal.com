Roof Cleaning & Soft Wash

Those Black Streaks Aren't Dirt.
They're Eating Your Shingles.

The dark staining you see on roofs across Greater Cincinnati is Gloeocapsa magma — a type of algae that feeds on the limestone in your shingles. Left alone, it breaks down the granule layer, reduces reflectivity, and shortens your roof's lifespan by years.

Moss and lichen are even worse. They retain moisture against the shingle surface, work their roots into the mat, and cause the kind of damage that voids warranties and triggers premature replacement.

Joe uses a low-pressure soft wash system — not a pressure washer. High-pressure washing destroys granules and can void manufacturer warranties. Soft wash uses a biodegradable treatment solution applied at low pressure that kills the organism at the root and prevents regrowth for years.

  • Low-pressure soft wash — safe for all shingle types
  • Algae, moss, and lichen treatment and removal
  • Black streak elimination (Gloeocapsa magma)
  • Gutter flush included
  • Biodegradable, plant-safe solution
  • Pre and post inspection — catch any hidden damage while we're up there

FAQ

Roof Cleaning Questions.

How often should a roof be cleaned?

In Greater Cincinnati's humid climate, most roofs benefit from cleaning every 3-5 years. North-facing slopes and roofs shaded by trees accumulate algae and moss faster and may need more frequent treatment.

Will soft wash damage my gutters or plants?

No. We use a biodegradable solution and rinse landscaping before and after application. The process is designed to be safe for everything around the house.

How long until I see results?

Black streaks from algae disappear within a few days as the dead organism rinses away with rain. Moss may take a few weeks to fully release from the surface.

Can you combine cleaning with an inspection?

Yes — and we recommend it. While Joe is up there cleaning, he does a full condition check. If there's any storm damage hiding under the staining, you'll know about it.
