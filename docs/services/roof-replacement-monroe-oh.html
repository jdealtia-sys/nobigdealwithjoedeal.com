Roof Replacement FAQ — Monroe, OH

How do I know if my Monroe home's builder-grade shingles need replacement? ▸
Homes built along the Monroe/I-75 corridor during the 2000s–2010s typically have builder-grade architectural shingles rated for 25–30 years. At the 20-year mark you should see a free inspection — look for granule loss in gutters, curling at edges, and any dark or bare spots. Joe's inspection is free and gives you an honest assessment, not a sales pitch.
Does hail damage affect whether I should replace or repair in Monroe? ▸
Monroe sits in the Butler County southwest storm corridor and sees regular hail. If your shingles are already aging and have hail damage on top of normal wear, replacement is often the right call — and your insurance may cover it. Joe inspects for both and helps you understand what your policy covers.
What's the typical cost for a roof replacement in Monroe, OH? ▸
Cost depends on square footage, pitch, current layer count, and any decking damage underneath. Monroe's 2000s suburban homes are typically straightforward ranch or two-story builds that come in at the lower end of the regional range. Joe provides a free, itemized estimate with no obligation.
How long does a Monroe roof replacement take? ▸
Most Monroe homes are completed in one full day. If there is significant decking damage or a multi-layer tear-off situation, the job may extend to two days. Joe schedules crew and materials in advance so the job isn't stretched unnecessarily.
