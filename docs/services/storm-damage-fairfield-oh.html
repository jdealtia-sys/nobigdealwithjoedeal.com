Storm Damage FAQ — Fairfield, OH

What should I do immediately after storm damage to my Fairfield, OH roof? ►
Document what you can safely see from ground level — photos, video. Do not climb on a wet or damaged roof. Call Joe for a free inspection before calling your insurance company, so you have independent documentation of the damage scope before an adjuster arrives.
How long do I have to file a storm damage claim in Ohio? ►
Ohio law and most policies allow one year from the date of loss, but some carriers use shorter windows for storm events. The sooner you document and file, the better. Joe can help you move quickly after a storm event.
Will filing a storm damage claim in Butler County raise my insurance rates? ►
A single weather-related claim typically does not raise rates — weather events are treated as acts of nature, not policyholder negligence. Multiple claims in a short window can affect rates. Joe gives you the honest picture before you file.
Does Joe cover wind damage as well as hail in Fairfield? ►
Yes. Wind damage — lifted shingles, missing sections, damaged ridgeline — is documented and claimed the same way as hail. Butler County's Great Miami River valley corridor sees consistent wind events. Joe inspects and documents both.
