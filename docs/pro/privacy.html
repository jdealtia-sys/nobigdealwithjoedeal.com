NBD Pro Privacy Policy

How NBD Pro collects, uses, and protects information — for the contractors who subscribe, the teams they invite, and the customer data they manage in the platform.

Last Updated: June 2026

This Privacy Policy describes how No Big Deal Home Solutions, a sole proprietorship owned and operated by Joe Deal in Cincinnati, Ohio ("NBD Pro," "we," "us," or "our"), handles information in connection with the NBD Pro software platform, the web and mobile apps at nobigdealwithjoedeal.com/pro, and related services (the "Service"). It is incorporated by reference into our Terms of Service.

NBD Pro is the contractor CRM and field-operations platform Joe built for his own roofing business and now offers to other contractors. This policy covers the Service. If you are a homeowner who contacted No Big Deal Home Solutions for a roofing estimate, the policy that applies to you is our home-services Privacy Policy instead.

1. Who This Policy Covers

This policy speaks to two groups:

  • Subscribers and their team ("you"). The contractor business that opens an account, the owner who subscribes, and the individual reps invited to team seats. Information about your account, billing, and use of the Service is covered here.
  • The customer data you manage. When you use NBD Pro, you load and create records about your own leads and customers — names, addresses, phone numbers, photos, estimates, and notes ("Your Data"). We process Your Data on your behalf so you can run your business; we do not use it for our own purposes. See Section 3.

2. Our Two Roles: Controller and Processor

NBD Pro handles information in two distinct roles, and it matters which one applies:

  • We are the controller of your account and billing information — the data you give us to create and pay for your subscription (name, business name, email, login, plan, and payment records). We decide how that information is used to run the Service, and this policy governs it.
  • We are a processor of Your Data — the leads, customers, and project records you put into the Service. For that data, you are the controller: it's your business's information about your own contacts, and you decide what to collect and how to use it. We store and process it under your instructions and our Terms, only to provide the Service to you.
Plain English: Your customer list is yours, not ours. We hold it so the software works. We don't sell it, we don't market to your customers, and we don't use it to train AI or build other products.

3. Information We Collect

Account & Profile Information

When you register or are invited to a team, we collect your name, email address, business/company name, and the password you set (authentication is handled by Google Firebase; we never see your password in plain text). If you sign up with Google, we receive your name and email from your Google account.

Billing Information

Paid plans are processed by Stripe. Stripe collects and stores your card and billing details; we do not store full payment card numbers on our servers. We retain billing records such as your plan, subscription status, invoices, the Stripe customer/subscription identifiers, and seat counts.

Your Data (Customer & Project Records)

As you use the Service, you create and upload records about your own business, which may include: lead and customer names, addresses, phone numbers and email addresses; property and roof information; photos and inspection notes; estimates, proposals, and documents; door-to-door territory and activity data; and messages you send through the Service. You control what you put in.

Usage & Technical Information

We automatically collect information needed to operate and secure the Service, including plan-usage counters (for example, leads and AI tool calls used this billing cycle), feature interactions, device and browser type, IP address, and diagnostic/error logs.

Cookies & Local Storage

We use essential cookies and browser local storage to keep you signed in and remember in-app preferences (such as your theme), and we use Google Analytics to understand aggregate usage. You can control cookies through your browser settings; disabling essential cookies may break the app.

4. How We Use Information

We use the information described above to:

  • Provide, operate, maintain, and secure the Service and your account;
  • Process subscriptions, seats, trials, and payments through Stripe, and send billing and transactional notices;
  • Provide the features you trigger — for example, sending an estimate, a text, or an email; pulling property data; or generating AI-assisted suggestions;
  • Respond to your support requests and communicate with you about the Service;
  • Monitor performance, detect and prevent fraud and abuse, and troubleshoot errors;
  • Improve the Service in the aggregate; and
  • Comply with legal obligations and enforce our Terms.

We do not sell your information or Your Data, and we do not use Your Data to advertise to your customers.

6. AI-Assisted Features

Some features use artificial intelligence (via the Anthropic Claude API) to draft estimates and proposals, summarize property information, and provide coaching suggestions. When you use these features, the relevant text and addresses you submit are sent to Anthropic for processing and returned to you. Under our API agreement, Anthropic does not use this data to train its models. AI output is assistive only and may be inaccurate — review it before relying on it or sharing it with a customer.

7. Your Responsibilities for the Data You Load

Because you are the controller of Your Data, you are responsible for collecting and using it lawfully. In particular:

  • You must have the rights and any required consents to load your contacts' information into the Service.
  • When you use the Service's texting, calling, or email tools, you are the sender. You are responsible for complying with the Telephone Consumer Protection Act (TCPA), CAN-SPAM, and similar laws — including obtaining and honoring consent and opt-outs from the people you contact.
  • You are responsible for the access you grant to team members and for removing access when someone leaves your team.

If one of your customers asks you to access or delete the information you hold about them, you can do that yourself inside the Service, because you control that data.

8. Data Retention & Deletion

We retain your account information and Your Data for as long as your account is active, and for a reasonable period afterward so you can export it. After that, it may be deleted in the ordinary course, except where we are required to keep certain records (for example, billing and tax records are typically retained for 7 years).

  • You can export Your Data while your account is active.
  • You may request deletion of your account and Your Data by contacting us; we will delete or anonymize it within a reasonable time, subject to legal retention requirements and short-term backup cycles.
  • Downgrading or cancelling does not immediately erase Your Data — see our Terms, Sections 5 and 7.

9. Data Security

We use reasonable, industry-standard measures to protect information, including:

  • Encryption of data in transit (HTTPS/TLS);
  • Storage on Google Firebase / Google Cloud infrastructure with access rules that scope each account's data to that account;
  • Restricted administrative access and security monitoring; and
  • PCI-compliant payment handling through Stripe.

No online system is perfectly secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security. Use a strong, unique password, keep your login private, and only invite team members you trust. Tell us promptly at jd@nobigdealwithjoedeal.com if you suspect unauthorized access.

10. Your Privacy Rights & Choices

Access, Correction & Export

You can view and update most of your account information in the app, and export Your Data while your account is active. For other requests, contact us at jd@nobigdealwithjoedeal.com.

State Privacy Rights (CCPA & others)

Depending on where you live, you may have rights to know what personal information we hold about you, to request its deletion, and to not be discriminated against for exercising those rights. California residents have these rights under the CCPA, and residents of states such as Virginia, Colorado, Connecticut, and Utah have similar rights. We honor verifiable requests under applicable law. Note that for information about your customers, the request usually belongs with you, the controller, who can act on it directly in the Service.

Communications

We send transactional and account messages required to operate the Service. Any optional product or marketing emails include an unsubscribe link.

Do Not Track

The Service does not currently respond to browser "Do Not Track" signals.

11. Where Your Data Is Hosted

The Service is hosted on Google Firebase / Google Cloud infrastructure located in the United States. By using the Service, you understand that your information is processed in the United States. We do not currently offer data residency in other regions.

12. Children's Privacy

The Service is a business tool intended for users who are at least 18 years old. It is not directed to children, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If you believe a child has provided information through the Service, contact us and we will delete it.

13. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. When we do, we will revise the "Last Updated" date above and, for material changes, make reasonable efforts to notify active subscribers. Your continued use of the Service after changes take effect constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.
